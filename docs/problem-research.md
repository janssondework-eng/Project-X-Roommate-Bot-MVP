# Problem Research

## Research Goal

The goal of this research is to understand the main problems students face when searching for housing and roommates.

Project "X" focuses on students who move to another city for university and need to find a room, apartment or compatible roommate.

## Key Problem

For students, housing search is often stressful, fragmented and risky.

Students have to search across many platforms, message many landlords, deal with broker commissions, check unreliable listings and guess whether future roommates will be compatible.

## Problems Identified

### 1. Landlords often reject students

Many landlords do not want to rent to students because of stereotypes about age, responsibility and apartment care.

The problem is that listings often do not mention this in advance. Students spend time contacting landlords only to receive a refusal later.

### 2. Listings are often incomplete or misleading

Listings may not include important details:

- real condition of the apartment
- condition of shared spaces
- internet availability
- actual move-in date
- real attitude toward students
- information about neighbors

This leads to wasted time and poor housing decisions.

### 3. High broker commissions

Many listings are posted by agents or brokers. For students with limited budgets, commission makes many housing options unaffordable.

### 4. Difficult roommate compatibility

Even if the housing option is acceptable, roommates may not be compatible.

Students often do not know in advance:

- whether roommates are noisy
- whether they smoke
- whether they keep common areas clean
- whether they invite guests
- whether their lifestyle is compatible

This creates conflicts after move-in.

### 5. Transport accessibility matters

Students need housing close to university, work, metro stations or public transport.

Cheap options may be far from transport, which increases daily stress and travel time.

### 6. Unclear move-in dates

Sometimes listings show a move-in date, but current tenants do not leave on time or the landlord changes plans.

This is especially problematic when the student needs housing urgently.

### 7. Lack of trust and safety

Informal housing chats do not provide enough structure or protection.

Users may face:

- fake profiles
- spam
- rude communication
- unreliable people
- unclear rental conditions

## Why Existing Platforms Are Not Enough

Large real estate platforms help find listings, but they do not fully solve student-specific problems.

They usually do not provide:

- student-focused filtering
- roommate compatibility
- lifestyle-based matching
- profile moderation
- mutual interest before contact
- profile verification
- structured roommate profiles

## Project "X" Approach

Project "X" addresses these problems through:

- structured student profiles
- moderation
- roommate preferences
- compatibility score
- matching explanation
- favorites
- mutual match system
- reports
- verified profiles
- admin analytics
- MVP report

## Main Hypothesis

Students need a dedicated, structured and safer way to find roommates and housing-related contacts.

A Telegram-based MVP can validate this hypothesis quickly and with low development cost.
